# Design System

## Overall style

Professional scientific research talk.

Avoid:
- stock art;
- decorative backgrounds;
- excessive borders;
- crowded notebook screenshots;
- titles that give away the reveal too early;
- more than two proof images per slide unless the slide is explicitly a synthesis.

Use:
- off-white background: `#FAFAF8`
- charcoal text: `#111111`
- muted grey subtitle: `#555555`
- failure/shortcut red: `#B23A48`
- stable/correct green: `#2E8B57`
- hidden-cue gold: `#D89C21`
- moon blue: `#4C78A8`
- star amber: `#F2A541`

## Typography

Use:
- Aptos, Avenir, Helvetica, or Arial
- Titles: 32-40 pt
- Subtitles: 18-22 pt
- Body: 18-22 pt
- Footnote/interpretation: 15-17 pt
- No body text below 14 pt

## Slide anatomy

Top:
- claim-style title

Middle:
- one main proof object
- one equation block if needed

Bottom:
- one short interpretation sentence

Speaker notes:
- detailed explanation goes here, not on the slide.

## Equation treatment

Equations are SVG/PNG assets rendered from LaTeX-style math.
Do not type complex equations manually in PowerPoint unless necessary.

## Animation treatment

For final PowerPoint:
- use MP4/H.264
- insert from local file
- set major reveals to play on click
- include first-frame PNG fallback
- final deck must be self-contained

## Layouts

1. Claim + equation
2. Claim + full-width evidence image
3. Two-panel counterfactual
4. Data audit
5. Heatmap caution
6. Intervention/re-test
7. Final synthesis

## Rule

Every slide must answer:

What is the claim?
What is the evidence?
What should the audience conclude?
